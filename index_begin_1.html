Purpose:

Purpose: You are being asked to take part in a research study. This study investigates how to make onboarding easier for newcomers to a project, who need to select an issue to tackle. Issues are problems or new needs reported by users, clients, or project maintainers.

This experiment is composed of two steps:

  • 1) Select an issue that fit for your skillset.

  • 2) Try to solve the issue.



Consent:

  • Activities: We will present you with a page with a list of issues; You are requested to select one of them according to your preference to start a contribution. After you will fill a survey reporting your experience.

  • Time: Your participation in this study is expected to last up to 60 minutes.

  • Confidentiality: It is possible that others could learn that you participated in this study, but the information you provide will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law.

  • Voluntary: Participation in this study is voluntary. You are free to decide not to participate or withdraw at any time without adversely affecting your relationship with the investigators.



Instructions:

  • The link "Begin Experiment" shows a list of issues from the JabRef project. You are going to select one issue to start contributing. You can see more information about the issue by clicking on its title.

  • Select one issue that would be best suitable for your skillset.

  • Please, do not navigate outside of the main and the issues pages to define which issue to contribute to. Use only those pages to decide whether you would be able to contribute to the issues.

  • When you PICK UP one issue, please, GO to the Development Environment and try to solve the issue.

  • We will use THINK ALOUD to understand how are you making decision in the contribution process. So, please say everything you are thinking to help us understand your decision making process. The section will be recorded for further research analysis.

  • In case of questions you can ask directly to the researcher that will supervise the experiment.



Begin Experiment:

  • Think Aloud. Every idea is important.
  • Select the link to start: BEGIN EXPERIMENT
